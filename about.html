Crafting the Extraordinary Since 1970

Range Rover was born from a single vision: to create a vehicle that combined the refinement and comfort of a luxury saloon with the off-road capability of a Land Rover. Over five decades later, that vision continues to redefine what an SUV can be.

Our Journey

Heritage Timeline

1970
The Original Classic
The first Range Rover rolled off the production line in Solihull, England. It featured a lightweight aluminium V8 engine, permanent four-wheel drive, and a split tailgate — innovations that changed the automotive world forever.
1981
4-Door Innovation
The introduction of the four-door Range Rover expanded its appeal to a global audience, blending family practicality with the unmistakable presence of the original design.
1994
Second Generation
The P38a brought electronic air suspension, traction control, and a more refined interior — cementing Range Rover's position as the world's most capable luxury vehicle.
2001
L322 & Modern Luxury
The third generation introduced monocoque construction, a BMW-derived V8, and a cabin that rivalled the finest executive sedans. Range Rover entered the 21st century as a true global luxury icon.
2012
All-Aluminium Revolution
The L405 featured the world's first all-aluminium SUV body, reducing weight by 420 kg while increasing strength. It redefined efficiency and performance in the luxury SUV segment.
2024
Electric Future
Range Rover continues to lead with electrified powertrains, sustainable materials, and autonomous-ready technology — shaping the future of luxury mobility while honouring its remarkable heritage.
